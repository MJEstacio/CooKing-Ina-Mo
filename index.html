Pork Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. This recipe uses pork as the main ingredient. Other proteins and seafood can also be used. Beef, shrimp, fish are commonly used to cook sinigang. The chicken version, on the other hand, is called sinampalukang manok. I prefer to use either pork belly or buto-buto when cooking sinigang.

Chicken Adobo

Easy Chicken Adobo is a straightforward adobong manok recipe wherein chicken pieces are cooked using the inadobo method without marinating the ingredients. This is my basic method in cooking adobo. It is what I always refer to when cooking this dish on a busy day.

Chicken Tinola

Chicken Tinola is a Filipino soup dish. It involves cooking chicken pieces in ginger broth. It is a popular cold weather dish. I always feel warm and cozy every time I eat it. This Filipino Chicken Soup is best enjoyed with fish sauce as dipping sauce, and a cup of warm white rice.

Pork Nilaga

Pork Nilaga is a type of Filipino pork soup in clear broth. I used pork belly in this version along with other ingredients such as potato, saba banana, and pechay (or baby bok choy). It is a perfect dish that you can enjoy during cold and rainy weather.

Bicol Express

Those who enjoy a dish that’s equal parts succulent, sweet, and spicy are sure to find a favorite in bicol express. The timeless combination of coconut milk, sharp chili peppers, and umami shrimp paste brings warmth and comfort to every bite. And who can forget about the mouthwatering, scrumptious pieces of chunky pork belly? Put these all together and you have a dish that’s beloved, not just by those in its native region, but all across the country! Bicol Express makes for the perfect meal to pair with heaping spoonfuls of rice, which will soak up that creamy, nutty sauce.

Hello There!

My name is Mark Joseph Estacio. I live in the Pasig City, Metro Manila, Philippines. I created this Filipino food blog, CooKing Ina Mo (The Filipino Taste), to share my best Filipino and Asian recipes to everyone. As huge fan of the Filipino way of cooking, it is my goal to teach people how to cook Filipino dishes using the simplest way possible. side for my deep passion in cooking and my continuous craving for the distinctive Filipino taste, I was motivated to create cooking videos in order to promote Filipino food. I noticed that Filipino Cuisine is not well represented outside of the Philippines. It is time for our food to be noticed, known, tasted, and enjoyed by the rest of the world.
